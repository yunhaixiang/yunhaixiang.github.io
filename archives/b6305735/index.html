Cohen's Forcing

Jan 26 2025 Articles a minute read (About 135 words)

“There is an old maxim that says that two empires that are too large will collapse. The analog in set theory is that two different theories that are too powerful must necessarily contradict each other.”

Saharon Shelah

Today I want to write about Cohen’s forcing in model theory and logic. Cohen’s forcing is a technique for showing the consistency and independence of first order statements. One example is Cohen’s proof of the independence of the axiom of choice and continuum hypothesis in Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory, which earned him a fields medal. The key to the technique is to extend certain models (which somehow somewhat reminds me of field extensions) by a so called “generic set”. This article is based on serveral notes I found on the internet.
